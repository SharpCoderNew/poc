1. Objective & Intent

The objective is to design, scope, and specify a fully responsive e-commerce website for fresh flowers, plants, and gifts that mirrors the shopping experience, features, and branding sensibilities of Teleflora.

Goals include: a fast, trust-worthy storefront; intuitive UX across desktop/tablet/mobile; a robust catalog with same-day delivery where available; strong branding and accessibility; scalable admin tooling and integrations for a florist network.

  • Provide a comprehensive, production-ready specification and design/technical blueprint covering architecture, data models, user journeys, UI/UX guidelines, CMS/admin tooling, integrations, performance & SEO practices, and edge-case coverage.
  • Deliver a blueprint that a development team can implement end-to-end with clear milestones and acceptance criteria.

2. Domain Context & Knowledge

Domain knowledge applied includes florist networks, freshness guarantees, substitutions policies, bouquet assembly, packaging standards, and local delivery logistics. It also encompasses commerce fundamentals (product catalogs, variants, pricing, promotions, same-day options), brand storytelling, and customer support touchpoints.

Assumptions
  • Target markets in English with USD; optional multi-language support in a future phase.
  • Headless CMS + e-commerce backend with secure payment integration.
  • PCI-DSS compliant payment handling and secure data storage.
Dependencies
  • Catalog and inventory management, promotions engine, delivery/logistics integrations.
  • Asset management for photography and media; analytics & marketing tech stack.
  • Hosting, CDN, and security tooling; accessibility and SEO tooling.

3. Inputs & Hidden Variables

  • Brand assets: logo, color palette, typography, photography guidelines.
  • Catalog data: categories, products, variants, descriptions, care notes, inclusions, multiple imagery per SKU, delivery area checks.
  • Promotions: calendars, discount rules, eligibility criteria.
  • Content: guides, FAQs, policy pages, translation workflows.
  • Localization: languages, currencies, regional tax rules, shipping restrictions.
  • Security/compliance artifacts: privacy notices, terms, accessibility conformance notes.
Hidden Variables
  • Seasonal demand patterns, partner capacity, substitutions policies.
  • Real-time inventory sync, delivery zone constraints, courier reliability.
  • Regulatory nuances, data retention, consent rules.
Data Flows

Illustrative data flows from product ingestion, promotions, checkout, and order tracking to the florist network and customers.

4. Constraints

  • Mobile-first, accessible (WCAG 2.1 AA), with performance budgets (LCP, CLS, TBT).
  • Semantic HTML, structured data (schema.org), sitemap and canonical URLs.
  • Secure by default: HTTPS, TLS, PCI-DSS-compliant payments, data minimization.
  • SEO-friendly: clean URLs, canonicalization, rich metadata, and robust internal linking.
  • Regulatory: GDPR/CCPA considerations, cookie consent, data retention policies.

  • Promotions engine with eligibility rules and schedule.

6. Success Criteria & Evaluation

Measure success with product, technical, and UX metrics. Acceptance criteria require documentation, contract sign-offs, and QA pass.

  • Product metrics: conversion rate, AOV, cart abandonment, repeat purchase rate, revenue.
  • Delivery metrics: same-day success rate, on-time delivery, window accuracy.
  • Technical: Core Web Vitals targets (LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, TTI/FID optimized).
  • Content & UX: WCAG checks, readability, image loading quality, branding consistency.

7. Edge Cases & Coverage

  • Substitutions and substitutions-override messaging for bouquets, inventory shortfalls.
  • Geographic delivery limits, weather disruptions, peak-season constraints.
  • Payment retries, fraud prevention, partial shipments, refunds & returns policy.
  • Localization fallbacks for missing translations, currency handling.

Coverage strategies include feature flags, rollback plans, and partner escalation paths with florist network.

8. Validation, Coherence & Governance

  • Cross-check data models against catalog flows, promotions, and delivery constraints.
  • Branding, design language, and content align with accessibility and SEO requirements.
  • APIs and data contracts for payments, logistics, and florist network are clearly defined.
  • Security & privacy controls implemented; compliance documentation prepared.

Output traceability: map each requirement to deliverables, validation steps, and acceptance criteria.
